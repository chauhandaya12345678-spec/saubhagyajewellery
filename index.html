Craftsmanship: How Every Piece Is Made

Base alloys are cast in reusable moulds, hand-finished with chisels and files, then micron-plated in matte or high-gloss gold. Stones — American Diamonds, Kundan, Polki, Meenakari enamels, cultured pearls — are hand-set one at a time by artisans who trained in traditional workshops in Jaipur, Coimbatore and Mumbai. Each piece then passes a five-point inspection (plating uniformity, stone seating, clasp fit, weight tolerance, finish sheen) before it is boxed. Photos on this website are taken of the actual production piece, not a stock render — what you see is what ships.
